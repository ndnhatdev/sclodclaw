# Trademark Policy

"RedClaw" and the RedClaw logo/wordmark are project marks used to identify the official runtime, documentation, release artifacts, and community materials published from `ndnhatdev/sclodclaw`.

## You may

- refer to RedClaw factually to describe compatibility, forks, integrations, benchmarks, or migration guides
- use the name in blog posts, tutorials, and comparisons that do not imply endorsement
- say your project is "compatible with RedClaw" if that statement is accurate

## You may not

- imply that your fork, package, image, domain, or organization is the official RedClaw project when it is not
- use the RedClaw name or logo in a way that is likely to confuse users about origin, sponsorship, or maintainership
- publish malware, credential harvesting, or deceptive fundraising under the RedClaw name

## Forks and derivatives

Forks are welcome under the repository license terms, but they should use clearly distinct branding if they materially diverge or are independently maintained.

Recommended wording:

- "Fork of RedClaw"
- "Based on RedClaw"
- "Compatible with RedClaw"

Avoid presenting a fork as the upstream project.

## Reporting misuse

If you find impersonation, misleading branding, or fraudulent distribution using the RedClaw name, open an issue in `https://github.com/ndnhatdev/sclodclaw` or contact the maintainers through the security policy when the report includes abuse or credential risk.
